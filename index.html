Chapter 7 vs. Chapter 13: Which Is Right for You?

Chapter 7 -- Fresh Start

Chapter 7 eliminates most unsecured debts (credit cards, medical bills, personal loans) in about 3-4 months. You must pass a means test based on your income.

  • Discharge in 3-4 months
  • Eliminates credit cards, medical debt
  • Must pass the means test
  • May lose non-exempt property
  • Filing fee: $338
Learn more about Chapter 7 →

Chapter 13 -- Payment Plan

Chapter 13 lets you keep your property while repaying debts over 3-5 years. Good for saving a home from foreclosure or a car from repossession. No means test required.

  • 3-5 year repayment plan
  • Keep your home and car
  • Catch up on missed payments
  • No means test requirement
  • Filing fee: $313

How Much Does Bankruptcy Cost in Rhode Island?

  • Chapter 7 filing fee: $338 (can request installment payments)
  • Chapter 13 filing fee: $313 (can be paid through your plan)
  • Attorney fees: Typically $1,200 to $2,200 for Chapter 7; $3,000 to $5,000 for Chapter 13 in Rhode Island
  • Credit counseling: $15-25 per course (two required)

Fee waivers are available for Chapter 7 filers whose income is below 150% of the federal poverty guidelines.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does bankruptcy cost in Rhode Island?

Chapter 7 filing fees are $338 and Chapter 13 filing fees are $313. Attorney fees in Rhode Island typically range from $1,200 to $2,200 for Chapter 7 and $3,000 to $5,000 for Chapter 13. You will also need to pay for two required credit counseling courses, usually $25-$50 total. Full cost breakdown.

Will I lose my house if I file bankruptcy in Rhode Island?

Rhode Island's homestead exemption protects up to $500,000 of equity in your home. If your equity is below that amount, you can keep your home in Chapter 7. In Chapter 13, you can keep your home while catching up on missed payments through your repayment plan. See all exemptions.

How long does bankruptcy take?

Chapter 7 typically takes 3-4 months from filing to discharge. Chapter 13 requires completing a 3-5 year repayment plan before receiving a discharge. The 341 meeting of creditors usually happens about 30 days after filing.

Should I file Chapter 7 or Chapter 13?

Chapter 7 eliminates most unsecured debt quickly but requires passing a means test. Chapter 13 allows you to keep property while repaying debts over 3-5 years. Your income, assets, and type of debt determine which chapter is best. Compare chapters.

Where do I file for bankruptcy in Rhode Island?

Rhode Island has 1 federal bankruptcy district: D.R.I.. You file in the district where you have lived for the greater part of the last 180 days.
