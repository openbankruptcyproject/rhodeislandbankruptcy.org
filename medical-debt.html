Medical Debt and Bankruptcy in Rhode Island

Medical debt is the leading cause of bankruptcy in America. If you are overwhelmed by medical bills in Rhode Island, bankruptcy can eliminate that debt and give you a fresh start.

This page provides general educational information, not legal advice. Consult a qualified attorney for advice about your specific situation.

Medical Debt Is Fully Dischargeable

Medical debt is classified as general unsecured debt under bankruptcy law. This means it receives no special protection and is typically eliminated entirely in bankruptcy.

  • Chapter 7: Medical debt is wiped out completely within 3-4 months of filing
  • Chapter 13: Medical debt is included in your repayment plan, and any unpaid balance is discharged at the end
  • No minimum amount: You can file bankruptcy whether you owe $5,000 or $500,000 in medical debt
  • All providers included: Hospital bills, doctor bills, ambulance fees, lab work, prescription drugs -- all dischargeable

Chapter 7 vs. Chapter 13 for Medical Debt

Chapter 7

  • Eliminates 100% of medical debt
  • Completed in 3-4 months
  • Must pass the means test
  • Non-exempt assets may be sold by trustee
  • Rhode Island exemptions: $500,000 homestead, $12,000 vehicle
  • Best for: people with primarily unsecured debt and limited assets

Chapter 13

  • Pay what you can afford over 3-5 years
  • Remaining medical debt discharged at end
  • No means test requirement
  • Keep all your property
  • Can also catch up on mortgage/car payments
  • Best for: people with assets to protect or higher income

Before Filing: Alternatives to Consider

  • Negotiate directly. Many hospitals offer significant discounts for uninsured patients or will reduce bills by 30-60% for prompt payment.
  • Financial assistance programs. Non-profit hospitals are required to have charity care policies. Ask about financial hardship applications.
  • Payment plans. Most providers will set up interest-free payment plans. Make sure any plan is affordable -- do not agree to payments you cannot sustain.
  • Statute of limitations. Medical debt has a limited collection window. If the debt is old, check whether it is still legally enforceable in Rhode Island.
  • Medical billing errors. Studies show a significant percentage of medical bills contain errors. Request itemized bills and dispute anything incorrect.

If these alternatives do not resolve your situation, bankruptcy remains a powerful tool for eliminating medical debt entirely.

Rhode Island-Specific Medical Debt Rules

Rhode Island follows the federal standard for wage garnishment: creditors can garnish the lesser of 25% of disposable earnings or the amount exceeding 30 times the federal minimum wage per week.

Medical providers who obtain a judgment against you in Rhode Island can use wage garnishment, bank levies, and property liens to collect. Filing bankruptcy stops all of these collection methods through the automatic stay.

Legal aid: Rhode Island Legal Services (rils.org) may offer free assistance with medical debt and bankruptcy.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can bankruptcy eliminate medical debt?

Yes. Medical debt is unsecured debt, which is typically fully dischargeable in both Chapter 7 and Chapter 13 bankruptcy. In Chapter 7, medical debt is eliminated entirely within 3-4 months. In Chapter 13, you may pay a portion through your plan, with the remainder discharged.

Can a hospital sue me for medical debt in Rhode Island?

Yes. Hospitals and medical providers can sue to collect unpaid medical bills in Rhode Island. If they win a judgment, they may be able to garnish your wages or levy your bank accounts. Filing bankruptcy stops all lawsuits and collection actions through the automatic stay.

Will bankruptcy stop medical debt wage garnishment?

Yes. The automatic stay immediately stops all wage garnishment, including garnishment for medical debt. In Rhode Island, Rhode Island follows the federal standard for wage garnishment: creditors can garnish the lesser of 25% of disposable earnings or the amount exceeding 30 times the federal minimum wage per week.

Should I file Chapter 7 or Chapter 13 for medical debt?

Chapter 7 is usually preferred for medical debt because it eliminates the debt completely in 3-4 months with no repayment. Chapter 13 is better if you have assets to protect that exceed Rhode Island's exemptions, or if you do not qualify for Chapter 7 under the means test.

Does medical debt affect my credit score?

Yes, but less than before. Since 2023, paid medical debt no longer appears on credit reports. Unpaid medical debt under $500 also no longer appears. Medical debt in collections still affects your score but is given less weight than other types of debt by newer scoring models.
