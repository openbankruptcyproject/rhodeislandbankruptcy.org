The 341 Meeting of Creditors in Rhode Island

The 341 meeting is one of the most anxiety-inducing parts of bankruptcy, but it is usually quick and straightforward. Here is what to expect in Rhode Island.

This page provides general educational information, not legal advice. Consult a qualified attorney for advice about your specific situation.

What Is the 341 Meeting?

The 341 meeting of creditors is a brief hearing required in every bankruptcy case under 11 U.S.C. section 341. Despite its name, creditors rarely attend. The meeting is conducted by the bankruptcy trustee assigned to your case -- not the bankruptcy judge.

In Rhode Island, meetings are held at U.S. Bankruptcy Court, 380 Westminster St, Providence, RI 02903. Many meetings are now conducted by phone or video conference. The typical meeting lasts 5-15 minutes.

Photo ID

Valid driver's license, state ID, or passport

Your Schedules

Copies of your bankruptcy filing for reference

Common Trustee Questions

The trustee will ask you questions under oath. Most are straightforward yes/no questions to verify the accuracy of your filing:

  1. Did you sign the petition and schedules, and is the information accurate?
  2. Did you list all of your assets -- including bank accounts, vehicles, real estate, and personal property?
  3. Did you list all of your debts?
  4. Have you sold, transferred, or given away any property in the last 2-4 years?
  5. Are you expecting any tax refunds, inheritance, or lawsuit proceeds?
  6. Is anyone holding property for you?
  7. Have you filed all required tax returns?
  8. Have you previously filed for bankruptcy?

Tips: Answer honestly and briefly. If you do not know the answer, say so. Do not guess. Your attorney will be present and can help if needed.

What Happens After

After Chapter 7 Meeting

  • Trustee may request additional documents
  • 60-day period for creditor objections
  • If no issues, discharge in about 60 days
  • Case closes shortly after discharge

After Chapter 13 Meeting

  • Plan confirmation hearing follows (30-45 days later)
  • Trustee may request plan modifications
  • Once confirmed, you make plan payments for 3-5 years
  • Discharge after completing all payments

Tips for Success

  • Arrive early. If in person, arrive 15 minutes before your scheduled time.
  • Dress appropriately. Business casual is fine. You do not need a suit.
  • Be honest. You are under oath. Lying to the trustee is a federal crime.
  • Be brief. Answer the question asked and stop. Do not volunteer extra information.
  • Review your schedules. Read through your filing the night before so the information is fresh.
  • Relax. The vast majority of 341 meetings are routine and uneventful.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the 341 meeting of creditors?

The 341 meeting (named after section 341 of the Bankruptcy Code) is a required hearing in every bankruptcy case. It is held about 30 days after filing and is conducted by the bankruptcy trustee, not the judge. In Rhode Island, meetings are held at U.S. Bankruptcy Court, 380 Westminster St, Providence, RI 02903 or by phone/video.

What questions will the trustee ask?

Common questions include: Did you list all your assets and debts? Is the information in your schedules accurate? Have you sold or transferred any property recently? Are you expecting any tax refunds, lawsuits, or inheritances? Did you read and understand the bankruptcy forms you signed?

Do I have to go in person?

In Rhode Island, many 341 meetings are conducted by phone or video since the COVID pandemic. Check with your attorney or the court clerk for the current format. If in person, meetings are held at U.S. Bankruptcy Court, 380 Westminster St, Providence, RI 02903.

Will creditors attend?

In most consumer cases, creditors do not attend. If a creditor does attend, they can ask you questions under oath. Your attorney will be present to object to improper questions.

What should I bring?

Bring: (1) a valid photo ID (driver's license or passport), (2) proof of Social Security number (SSN card, tax return, or W-2), (3) copies of your bankruptcy schedules, and (4) any documents your attorney has requested.

What happens after the 341 meeting?

After the 341 meeting, the trustee may request additional documents or information. In Chapter 7, creditors have 60 days to object to discharge. In Chapter 13, the confirmation hearing follows. If no issues arise, your case proceeds toward discharge.
