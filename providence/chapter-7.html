Chapter 7 Bankruptcy in Providence, Rhode Island

Chapter 7 is the fastest form of bankruptcy relief. Most unsecured debts are eliminated in 3-4 months. Here is how it works for Providence residents filing in the D.R.I..

This page provides general educational information, not legal advice. Consult a qualified attorney for advice about your specific situation.

What Is Chapter 7?

Chapter 7 bankruptcy (also called "liquidation" bankruptcy) eliminates most unsecured debts -- credit cards, medical bills, personal loans, and old utility bills. In exchange, non-exempt assets may be sold to pay creditors. However, most Chapter 7 cases are "no-asset" cases where the filer keeps everything.

In Rhode Island, you can protect up to $500,000 in home equity and $12,000 in vehicle equity. Additional exemptions cover household goods, clothing, retirement accounts, and more.

Do You Qualify? The Means Test

To file Chapter 7 in Rhode Island, you must pass the means test. This compares your household income to the Rhode Island median.

Household SizeAnnual Median Income
1 person$63,147
2 persons$80,655
3 persons$97,449
4 persons$119,467

If your income is below the median for your household size, you qualify automatically. If above, a detailed expense calculation may still allow you to file.

Chapter 7 Timeline in Providence

1File petition with the D.R.I.

Your attorney files your petition, schedules, and means test. The automatic stay immediately stops collections, garnishments, and lawsuits.

2341 Meeting of Creditors (30 days)

You attend a brief meeting of creditors where the trustee asks questions under oath about your finances.

3Discharge (60-90 days after 341)

If no objections are filed, you receive your discharge order eliminating qualifying debts.

What Debts Does Chapter 7 Eliminate?

Dischargeable

  • Credit card debt
  • Medical bills
  • Personal loans
  • Old utility bills
  • Most old tax debts
  • Deficiency balances

Not Dischargeable

  • Student loans (usually)
  • Child support / alimony
  • Recent tax debts
  • Debts from fraud
  • DUI injury debts
  • Government fines

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I qualify for Chapter 7 in Providence?

You must pass the means test. If your household income is below the Rhode Island median, you qualify automatically. If above, a detailed expense calculation determines eligibility.

How long does Chapter 7 take in Providence?

Typically 3-4 months from filing to discharge. The 341 meeting of creditors is usually 30 days after filing.

What debts does Chapter 7 eliminate?

Chapter 7 eliminates most unsecured debts including credit cards, medical bills, personal loans, and old utility bills. It does not eliminate student loans (usually), child support, alimony, most tax debts, or debts from fraud.

Will I lose my car in Chapter 7 in Rhode Island?

Rhode Island's vehicle exemption protects up to $12,000 of equity in your car. If your car equity is below that amount, you keep it.
