Bankruptcy FAQ for Providence, Rhode Island

Answers to the most common questions about filing bankruptcy in Providence.

This page provides general educational information, not legal advice. Consult a qualified attorney for advice about your specific situation.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I file bankruptcy in Providence?

You file a bankruptcy petition with the D.R.I.. Most people hire an attorney, though you can file pro se. Before filing, you must complete a credit counseling course.

Should I file Chapter 7 or Chapter 13 in Providence?

Chapter 7 eliminates most unsecured debt in 3-4 months but requires passing a means test. Chapter 13 lets you repay debts over 3-5 years while keeping property. Your income, assets, and goals determine the best choice.

Will bankruptcy stop wage garnishment?

Yes. The automatic stay takes effect immediately when you file and stops all garnishments, lawsuits, and collection calls.

How long does bankruptcy stay on my credit report?

Chapter 7 stays on your credit report for 10 years. Chapter 13 stays for 7 years. However, many people see their credit scores begin to recover within 1-2 years of filing.

Can I keep my car if I file bankruptcy in Rhode Island?

Rhode Island's vehicle exemption protects up to $12,000 of equity. If your equity is within the exemption, you keep the car. In Chapter 13, you continue making payments through your plan.

Can I keep my house if I file bankruptcy in Rhode Island?

Rhode Island's homestead exemption protects up to $500,000 of equity. In Chapter 13, you can catch up on missed mortgage payments through your plan.

Can I file bankruptcy on medical bills?

Yes. Medical debt is unsecured debt that is dischargeable in both Chapter 7 and Chapter 13 bankruptcy.

Do I have to go to court?

You must attend the 341 meeting of creditors, which is usually held at a meeting room (not a courtroom). Your attorney handles most court appearances.

Can I file bankruptcy if I am married?

Yes. You can file individually or jointly with your spouse. Filing jointly saves on attorney fees and filing costs.

Can I file bankruptcy again?

After a Chapter 7 discharge, wait 8 years for another Chapter 7 or 4 years for Chapter 13. After a Chapter 13 discharge, wait 2 years for another Chapter 13 or 6 years for Chapter 7. Use the 1328(f) screener at 1328f.com to check eligibility.
