Filing Bankruptcy in East Providence

Residents of East Providence (Providence County, population approximately 48,000) file bankruptcy in the D.R.I.. The same Rhode Island exemptions apply to all filers in the state.

Key Rhode Island exemptions:

  • Homestead: $500,000 of equity in your home
  • Vehicle: $12,000 of equity in your car
  • Retirement: Unlimited for ERISA-qualified plans

Chapter 7 vs. Chapter 13

Chapter 7

  • Eliminate most unsecured debt
  • Takes 3-4 months
  • Must pass means test
  • Filing fee: $338

Chapter 13

  • Repay debts over 3-5 years
  • Keep all property
  • No means test
  • Filing fee: $313

Frequently Asked Questions

Where do East Providence residents file for bankruptcy?

Residents of East Providence, Rhode Island file in the D.R.I.. You file in the district where you have lived for the greater part of the last 180 days.

How much does bankruptcy cost in East Providence?

Filing fees are the same across Rhode Island: $338 for Chapter 7 and $313 for Chapter 13. Attorney fees in the Providence area typically range from $1,200 to $2,200 for Chapter 7.

What exemptions apply in East Providence?

East Providence residents use Rhode Island state exemptions: homestead $500,000, vehicle $12,000.
