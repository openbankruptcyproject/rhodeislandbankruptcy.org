Frequently Asked Questions

How much does bankruptcy cost in Providence, Rhode Island?

Chapter 7 filing fees are $338 and Chapter 13 filing fees are $313. Attorney fees in Providence typically range from $1,200 to $2,200 for Chapter 7 and $3,500 to $5,000 for Chapter 13.

Will I lose my house if I file bankruptcy in Providence?

Rhode Island's homestead exemption protects up to $500,000 of equity in your home. If your equity is below that amount, you can keep your home in Chapter 7. In Chapter 13, you keep your home while catching up on missed payments.

Where do I file for bankruptcy in Providence?

Bankruptcy cases in Providence are filed in the D.R.I.. The courthouse is located at 380 Westminster St, Providence, RI 02903.

How long does bankruptcy take in Providence?

Chapter 7 typically takes 3-4 months from filing to discharge. Chapter 13 requires completing a 3-5 year repayment plan before receiving a discharge.
