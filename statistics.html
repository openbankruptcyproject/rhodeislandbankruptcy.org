Key Facts About Bankruptcy in Rhode Island

  • Homestead exemption: $500,000 -- Rhode Island's homestead exemption protects up to $500,000 of equity in your primary residence.
  • Chapter 13 dismissal risk: Nationally, about one-third to two-fifths of Chapter 13 cases end in dismissal. Quality of legal representation is one of the strongest predictors of plan completion.
  • Prior filer rate: Across the nation, roughly 25-30% of Chapter 13 filers had a prior bankruptcy case, suggesting many debtors cycle through the system without achieving lasting relief.
  • Means test: Rhode Island's median income for a single filer is approximately $55,000/year.

Understanding the Data

Chapter 13 plans run 3-5 years. Cases filed in recent years (2020-2024) are still active if succeeding -- only early dismissals have been counted. The most reliable dismissal rates come from mature years (2008-2019) where most cases have reached final outcomes.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many bankruptcy cases are filed in Rhode Island?

Since 2008, Rhode Island's district has processed over 25,000+ bankruptcy cases. Filing volumes peaked around 2010-2011 following the financial crisis and have generally declined since.

What is the Chapter 13 success rate?

Nationally, about 33-40% of Chapter 13 cases are dismissed before completion. Rates vary by district and depend on factors including the local legal market, judicial practices, and the complexity of cases filed. National dismissal rate data.
