Bankruptcy Filing Checklist for Rhode Island

Filing bankruptcy requires careful preparation. Use this comprehensive checklist to gather everything you need before filing in Rhode Island.

This page provides general educational information, not legal advice. Consult a qualified attorney for advice about your specific situation.

Income Documents

  • Last 6 months of pay stubs (all jobs)
  • Last 2 years of federal tax returns (filed copies)
  • Last 2 years of Rhode Island state tax returns
  • Current year income documentation (W-2s, 1099s)
  • Social Security or disability income statements
  • Unemployment compensation documentation
  • Pension or retirement income statements
  • Rental income documentation
  • Child support or alimony received
  • Any other sources of income

Asset Documents

  • Bank statements (all accounts, last 6 months)
  • Vehicle titles or registration for all vehicles
  • Most recent mortgage statement
  • Home value estimate (Zillow, Redfin, or appraisal)
  • Retirement account statements (401k, IRA, pension)
  • Life insurance policies (cash value)
  • Investment and brokerage account statements
  • List of personal property with estimated values
  • Business ownership documents (if applicable)
  • Any property held by others on your behalf

Debt Documents

  • List of all creditors with names, addresses, account numbers, and balances
  • Recent statements for all credit cards
  • Medical bills and collection notices
  • Student loan statements
  • Auto loan statements
  • Personal loan documentation
  • Judgments, liens, or garnishment orders
  • Tax debts (IRS notices, state tax notices)
  • Past-due utility bills
  • Payday loan documentation
  • Any debts from lawsuits or legal disputes

Required Steps Before Filing

1

Credit Counseling

Complete an approved credit counseling course within 180 days before filing. Cost: $15-$50. Available online.

2

Credit Reports

Pull free reports from AnnualCreditReport.com to verify all debts are listed.

3

Means Test

Calculate if you qualify for Chapter 7 based on Rhode Island median income.

Rhode Island Exemption Quick Reference

Key Rhode Island Exemptions

  • Homestead: $500,000
  • Vehicle: $12,000
  • Federal exemptions: Not available (must use state exemptions)

Filing Fees

  • Chapter 7: $338
  • Chapter 13: $313
  • Fee waiver: Available for income below 150% of poverty level
  • Installments: Court may allow payment in installments

Where to File in Rhode Island

Court: U.S. Bankruptcy Court, 380 Westminster St, Providence, RI 02903

Districts: Rhode Island has the District of Rhode Island. You file in the district where you have lived for the greater part of the last 180 days.

Legal assistance: Rhode Island Legal Services (rils.org) may provide free or reduced-cost help with your filing.

Frequently Asked Questions

What documents do I need to file bankruptcy in Rhode Island?

You will need: last 6 months of pay stubs, last 2 years of tax returns, bank statements for all accounts, a list of all debts with account numbers and balances, vehicle titles or registration, mortgage statements, and a list of all property you own with estimated values.

How long does it take to prepare a bankruptcy filing?

Gathering documents and completing the required credit counseling course typically takes 1-4 weeks. The actual filing can be done in a day once everything is prepared.

Do I need a lawyer to file bankruptcy?

While you can file pro se (without a lawyer), bankruptcy is complex. Mistakes can cost you property, result in case dismissal, or lead to debts not being discharged. Rhode Island Legal Services (rils.org) may provide free or low-cost assistance if you cannot afford an attorney.

What is the means test?

The means test compares your household income to Rhode Island's median income. If your income is below the median, you automatically qualify for Chapter 7. If above, additional calculations determine eligibility. The median income varies by household size.

How much does it cost to file bankruptcy?

Chapter 7 filing fee: $338. Chapter 13 filing fee: $313. Attorney fees vary but typically range from $1,000-$2,000 for Chapter 7 and $2,500-$5,000 for Chapter 13. You may be able to pay in installments or qualify for a fee waiver.
